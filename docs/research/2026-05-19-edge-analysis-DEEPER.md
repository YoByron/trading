## 0. What's different from the first pass

The first pass tested 4 single-dim families and K=14 cells. It found
Thursday (raw p=0.014, adj_p=0.190) as the best slice and concluded no
edge survived Bonferroni at α=0.05.

This pass widens the search:

- Adds **week-of-month** and **calendar-month** single-dim families.
- Adds three **2D interactions**: day-of-week × DTE, day-of-week ×
  hold-time, month × DTE.
- Adds **sequence/autocorrelation**: win-rate conditional on the prior
  chronological trade's outcome.

**Cluster-day analysis (entries the day after SPY moved > 1 ATR) was
SKIPPED.** `yfinance` is not installed in this environment, and the
local `data/historical/SPY_*.csv` files only cover Nov 2025 — they do
not span the Jan 22 – Apr 15 2026 trading window. Including this
dimension would have required network calls and is deferred.

## 8. Plain-English verdict

> **No conditional slice in `data/trades.json` shows a defensible
> positive edge after correct multi-comparison correction.** Zero of
> 39 tested cells survived Bonferroni at α=0.05. The cohort has
> realized expectancy −$57.36 / trade, profit factor 0.22, and a
> 12-trade consecutive losing streak. The strongest in-sample
> directional signal — Thursday entries (in three different cuts) —
> has Bonferroni-adjusted p ≥ 0.53 and is therefore consistent with
> chance given the size of the search.

## 9. Recommendation

Two acceptable paths forward. Pick one explicitly; do not blend.

### (a) Restart the 30-trade validation cohort with a *single*, written hypothesis change

Per `.claude/rules/controlled-experiment.md` and
`.claude/rules/kill-criteria.md`, the validation cohort must be
redesigned with a written hypothesis change before adding any more
trades. The strongest in-sample candidate is **Thursday-only entries
in the 31-45 DTE bucket** (Thu | 31-45: n=9, 55.6% win rate, +$3.78
expectancy). This is **not proof of edge** — it is the one slice
internally consistent across three independent cuts of the data. If
the operator chooses to test it, the new cohort must:

- Trade Thursdays only, 31-45 DTE, 1-lot, 24h-min-hold.
- Be a fresh 30-trade cohort. The 69 historical trades are training
  data and may not be counted toward kill-criteria gating of the new
  cohort.
- Be killed if expectancy ≤ 0 over 30 closed trades, profit factor
  ≤ 1.0, win rate < break-even, 3 consecutive max-loss stops, or 10%
  drawdown — the existing kill criteria apply unchanged.

The honest expectation for this cohort is **modest**: even if the
in-sample 55.6% Thursday win rate were the true mean, the realized
break-even win rate at the cohort's avg-win/avg-loss of $70.50 /
−$97.81 is **58.1%** (`avg_loss / (avg_win + avg_loss)`). The
candidate slice's point estimate is *below* the break-even line. The
hypothesis test would need *both* a real day-of-week edge *and* a
favorable shift in avg-win-vs-avg-loss to be profitable.

### (b) Structural pause — redesign the strategy itself

Defensible given the evidence:

- 53/69 trades closed in < 24h — the strategy collected almost no theta.
  With the new 24h-min-hold rule, the *behavior* of the system in the
  next cohort will not resemble what generated this dataset, which
  partially invalidates *any* slice derived from it.
- Average win ($70.50) is smaller than average loss ($97.81). Even at
  80% win rate the strategy needs the loss tail to compress before it
  pays. A structural change candidate is **debit verticals** (winners
  larger than losers, lower win rate) or **a different DTE band**
  (e.g., 0-7DTE for higher theta capture, with regime gating).
- A regime gate (e.g., do not enter when realized vol > X percentile)
  is also a structural change worth testing.

A structural pause means: stop adding entries until the new strategy
spec is written and committed (separate PR), then start a fresh 30-trade
validation cohort under the rewritten rules.

**The author's recommendation, given that path (a)'s candidate is
point-estimate below break-even, is path (b): structural redesign.**
Path (a) is acceptable only if the operator explicitly accepts the
math above.

## 10. Honest call-outs

1. **Sample is small.** 69 closed trades, with the best in-sample
   single cell at n=8. Wilson lower bound on Thu | <24h is 30.6% —
   still below the 58.1% break-even win rate. The point estimate
   exceeds break-even; the confidence interval does not.
2. **K=39 is the honest count.** It includes all cells with n≥5
   across the 10 families analyzed. Reducing K by hand-picking
   "interesting" families is p-hacking.
3. **The "Thursday" story may itself be a confound.** Thursday trades
   may correlate with specific expiry-cycle distance (close to
   Friday weekly expiry) or with weekly-options selection that is
   not visible in the file's `legs.expiry` field.
4. **Sequence direction is suggestive, untested:** after-loss win
   rate (17.6%) is below baseline (23.2%), after-win is above (31.2%).
   That is a 13.6-point spread but raw p only 0.306. Worth re-checking
   once the validation cohort has n≥30 cleanly.
5. **Cluster-day analysis was skipped**, not "tested and found
   negative." Future work: install `yfinance` (or use the local
   `data/historical/` infra extended to Jan-Apr 2026) and add an
   `entry_day_atr_quintile` dimension.
6. **Field coverage limits:** `target_delta` populated for only 2/69
   rows; `exit_reason` populated for 0/69 rows. We could not slice on
   realized short delta or exit reason. Those dimensions are
   structurally unavailable from this file and would require sourcing
   from Alpaca's order history or rerunning entries with proper
   journaling.

## 11. Bottom line

> Going from K=14 to K=39 by adding 2D interactions and sequence
> effects did not surface a multi-comparison-robust edge. The
> Thursday story is the most internally consistent directional
> signal in the file (3 different cuts agree on the sign), but it
> does not clear Bonferroni and its point estimate is still below
> the realized break-even win rate. **Recommended action: structural
> redesign, not Thursday-only validation.** If the operator chooses
> Thursday-only validation anyway, the math above (break-even 58.1%,
> candidate point estimate 55.6%) makes that cohort more likely to
> kill the strategy than to confirm it.
