## 4. Multi-comparison correction

We computed K = 14 buckets with n ≥ 5 (the only buckets large enough to be tested honestly). Across all of them, the minimum raw p-value is the Thursday bucket: p = **0.0136**.

Bonferroni-corrected for K=14 tests: **adj_p = 0.0136 × 14 = 0.190**.

| Test family | min-p bucket | n | wins | raw p | Bonferroni adj_p (K=14) |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| day-of-week | Thu | 10 | 6 | 0.0136 | 0.190 |

**Verdict on the Thursday hypothesis at α = 0.05:**

> **Not supported.** The raw effect is suggestive (Thursday's point win rate of 60% beats the 23.2% baseline at raw p=0.014), but after Bonferroni correction for the 14 buckets examined the adjusted p-value is 0.190, which fails the α=0.05 bar. Equivalently: a Thursday-shaped bump can plausibly arise by chance when you slice 14 buckets and pick the best one.

The directional claim ("Mon/Tue/Fri win rates below 20%") *is* descriptively true on this sample, but the implication ("therefore Thursday entry is a real edge") **does not survive a basic look-elsewhere correction**.

## 5. Honest call-outs

1. **Small n per cell.** Thursday n=10. Wednesday n=11. Wilson lower bound on Thursday is 31.3% — that is still below the 50% break-even win rate implied by the cohort's avg_win $70.50 / avg_loss $97.81 (break-even ≈ 58.1%). The "Thursday is profitable" claim is right on the edge even at the point estimate.
2. **Look-back / data-dredging bias.** We chose to test day-of-week, DTE, hold time, and credit/width — four families. K=14 buckets actually tested. Any future "Thursday-only" rule conditioned on this dataset is fitted to the past, not validated.
3. **No out-of-sample test.** All 69 trades are training data. None have been held out for confirmation.
4. **Confounding.** Thursday entries may have, on average, different DTE (e.g., 1DTE for the next-day Friday expiry) or different hold-time profiles, and may correlate with specific market regimes during Jan – May 2026. We did not control for these.
5. **Missing `target_delta` (only 2/69 rows).** We could not verify whether Thursday trades were structurally different (delta, width, credit) from other days.
6. **One-sided tests only.** The Tuesday p=0.989 reflects how *bad* Tuesday is — Tuesday's expectancy −$170.65 is the largest single-day drag and may itself be the real signal (avoid Tuesdays), not a Thursday edge.

## 6. Recommendation for the next 30-trade validation cohort

Per `kill-criteria.md` and `controlled-experiment.md`, the experiment is 30 paper trades; one structure per day, 1-lot. Given the data above:

- **Do NOT trade a "Thursday-only" rule as the validation slice.** The Bonferroni-corrected p value of 0.190 does not clear α=0.05. Trading Thursday-only on this evidence would be reading randomness as signal.
- **The most defensible signal in the data is negative**, not positive: Tuesday entries had expectancy −$170.65 (n=17), Mon/Fri/Wed all negative expectancy. The honest action is to **avoid Tuesday entries** during the next 30-trade cohort and let entries fall naturally across Mon/Wed/Thu/Fri, then re-test.
- **Expected (not projected) outcome of an "avoid Tuesday" 30-trade cohort**, based purely on historical Mon/Wed/Thu/Fri pooled win rate: pooled n = 15+11+10+16 = 52, wins = 2+4+6+3 = 15 → 28.8% win rate (Wilson 95% CI [18.2%, 42.4%]), pooled expectancy = (−460−173+75−499)/52 = **−$20.33/trade**. Still negative, just less negative.
- **Falsification target for the next cohort** (consistent with `kill-criteria.md`):
  - kill if expectancy ≤ 0 after 30 closed validation trades
  - kill if profit factor ≤ 1.0
  - kill if win rate < 58% (the realized break-even with current avg_win/avg_loss)
  - kill on 3 consecutive max-loss stops
- **Most actionable structural finding**: 53/69 trades closed in <24h. With the new 24h-min-hold rule and 1-trade/day cap, those 53 trades cease to exist as a pattern. The "Thursday effect" may be a side effect of when the same-day-close intraday system happened to fire on Thursdays; with the new hold-time floor, this slice will not reproduce.

## 7. Bottom line

> The 69-trade cohort does **not** demonstrate a real, multi-comparison-robust day-of-week edge. Thursday's 60% win rate (n=10, raw p=0.014) is the best-looking slice but fails Bonferroni correction at K=14 (adj_p=0.190). Realized cohort expectancy is −$57.36/trade with a 12-trade losing streak. **No conditional slice produced a statistically defensible positive edge.** Run the 30-trade validation per `kill-criteria.md`; do not pre-condition entries on day-of-week.
